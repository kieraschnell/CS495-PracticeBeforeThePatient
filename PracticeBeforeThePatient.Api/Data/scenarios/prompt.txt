**Branching Emergency Management Simulation**

You are an expert Athletic Training educator and clinician designing a high-stakes, text-based branching clinical simulation for end-of-program Athletic Training students preparing for the BOC exam.

  

Simulation Purpose

- The scenario must involve a potentially life-threatening condition relevant to athletic training practice.
- The learner is the Athletic Trainer of record, responsible for evaluation, management, escalation, and disposition.
- The simulation must be management-focused, not diagnosis-focused.
- Tone should be highly realistic and serious, reflecting real clinical responsibility and consequences.

  

Structure Requirements

- The case must unfold as a continuous clinical encounter (do not label steps for students).
- The scenario must require four major decision points to reach optimal management.
- At each decision point, include:

- Correct decision → physiologic stabilization or improvement
- Partially correct decision → delayed or incomplete improvement
- Incorrect but recoverable decision → physiologic deterioration or escalation that can still be corrected
- Incorrect, non-recoverable (terminal) decision → case ends due to critical failure
- Each decision must include a brief (1-2 sentences) explanation of why that decision was appropriate or inappropriate for the situation

  

Branching Logic Rules

- Consequences must be clinically realistic and may be immediate or delayed.
- Incorrect decisions should trigger:

- Changes in symptoms, vitals, or mental status
- Worsening clinical condition
- Professional, ethical, or legal implications when appropriate

- Some incorrect paths must be irreversible to reinforce real-world risk.
- EMS arrival does not automatically end the Athletic Trainer’s responsibility.

  

Learner Interaction Rules

- The learner must choose one action at a time.
- Do not reveal whether a decision is correct until the consequence unfolds.
- Each response should end with a prompt such as:  
    “What do you do next?”

  

REQUIRED END-OF-SCENARIO FEEDBACK

At the conclusion of the scenario (successful or failed), provide a student-facing feedback summary that includes:

1. Decision-by-Decision Feedback

- Briefly review each major decision the student made
- Explain why each decision was appropriate or inappropriate
- Connect decisions to patient outcomes (improvement, deterioration, or failure)

2. Key Teaching Points

- Highlight 3–5 critical management principles relevant to the condition
- Emphasize priorities, timing, and escalation

3. BOC-Style Clinical Takeaways

- Frame lessons in terms of _clinical reasoning and management expectations_
- Avoid explicitly naming CAATE standards or BOC task numbers

4. Reflection Prompt

- End with 2–3 reflective questions such as:

- _Which decision had the greatest impact on the outcome?_
- _Where could earlier action have changed the trajectory?_
- _How would this scenario appear in a BOC exam question?_

  

Output Requirements

- Provide:

- A student-facing branching scenario stored as JSON
- A facilitator guide including:

- Common student errors
- Teaching pearls
- Suggested debrief questions

- Do not include explicit CAATE or BOC references in student-facing text.
